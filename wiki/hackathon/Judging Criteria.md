# Judging Criteria

**Official rubric.** Source: Discord `#ewaluacja-punktów` (Adrian Romański, 30 Jun
2026) + the released **[[Hackaton deliverables]]** and **[[Hackaton task]]** (read
2026-07-04; translated from Polish).

At the final hackathon your team faces a **judging panel playing Client +
Potential Investor**. You're scored on how effectively you used each day's
knowledge to turn a raw problem into a **finished, market-ready, working product**.

## ⚠️ [[Criterion Zero]] (necessary condition — a gate, not points)
> The app **must fulfil the assigned domain task**, with every step running as
> **real, inspectable logic — not one prompt dressed up to look structured**. If it
> doesn't genuinely solve the problem, the project **fails Client verification** —
> disqualified before points are counted.

**The task is now known** ([[Hackaton task]]): build an **R&D inventive-problem
solver** that takes an assigned problem (one of 7 UN-SDG challenges), reformulates it
as a **technical contradiction**, generates **≥3 candidates via TRIZ** *and* **≥3 via
a second method of our choice**, **evaluates** all candidates against the problem,
**selects** one, and presents the full **reasoning trail** (problem → contradiction →
candidates → evaluation → choice). ≥2 concept-generation methods, **one MUST be TRIZ**.

## Scoring — three layers

### A. Per-day deliverables — 100 pts (5 × 20)
Each is a concrete artifact submitted via the form ([[Hackaton deliverables]]); owned
by the judge who taught that day ([[People]]).
| # | Day | Max | Deliverable |
|---|-----|-----|-------------|
| 1 | Product Design & MVP | 20 | Business process analysis — **BPMN or [[Event Storming]]** (Miro / readable screenshot) |
| 2 | UI/UX & A11Y | 20 | **Design System** with correct **Design Tokens** (color, type, spacing, radii, shadows) + interface consistency |
| 3 | Angular, Nx, Debugging | 20 | Repo link with **working code + run instructions** |
| 4 | Fullstack, Architecture, LLM Eng | 20 | **Evaluation report**: input scenarios → idea evaluation → metrics |
| 5 | Deployment, Scale & Optimization | 20 | Link to the **deployed, public, working app** |

**Gate to pitch:** artifacts from *each* day + team roster & responsibilities
(ideally an **ng-diagram**).

### B. Pitch & peer rating — 50 pts
- **Pitch [25]** — 5 min, hard cutoff (interrupted mid-sentence), **no Q&A**. Slides
  recommended; English slides suggested (one anglophone judge).
- **Rate all teams [25]** — you only get these points if a teammate rates **every**
  team.

### C. General judged categories — 50 pts (each judge, on top of artifacts)
| Category | Max | What it rewards |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| **Innovation** | 25 | originality/creativity; unconventional approach; stands out; real-impact potential |
| **Usability** | 10 | practical, intuitive; genuinely solves the user's problem in everyday use |
| **Design** | 10 | pure visual layer — is it polished & nice to look at |
| **Completeness** | 5 | fully delivered & launch-ready, not a half-finished prototype |

## What this means for us
- **Balance beats specialization** across the day-pillars — a frontend-only team caps
  low and risks [[Criterion Zero]].
- **Innovation is the single biggest bucket (25)** — the *second* concept-generation
  method (beside TRIZ) and a real, inspectable reasoning trail are our best shot at it.
- **Cheap, under-invested points:** A11Y (Day 2), Deployment (Day 5), and the
  easy-to-forget **peer rating (25)** — assign one teammate to rate all teams.
- **Pitch to "Client + Investor"** — business value + market readiness, not just tech.

We score ourselves against the day-pillars — see [[Eval Suite]] / [[Scoreboard]].
(Layers B & C are judge-side; not yet modelled in the eval suite.)
