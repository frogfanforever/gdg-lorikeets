# Judging Criteria

**Official rubric.** Source: Discord `#ewaluacja-punktów`, posted by judge Adrian
Romański on 30 Jun 2026 (scraped 2026-07-02; translated from Polish).

At the final hackathon your team faces a **judging panel playing Client +
Potential Investor**. You're scored on how effectively you used each day's
knowledge to turn a raw problem into a **finished, market-ready, working product**.

## ⚠️ [[Criterion Zero]] (necessary condition — a gate, not points)
> The app **must fulfil the assigned domain task**. AI tools and algorithms must
> correctly and logically process the problem and return substantively correct,
> sensible solutions. If it doesn't solve the assigned problem, the project **fails
> Client verification** — disqualified before points are counted.

## Scoring: 100 pts = 5 pillars × 20 (one per workshop day)
| # | Pillar | Day | Max |
|---|--------|-----|-----|
| 1 | Product Design & MVP | 1 | 20 |
| 2 | UI/UX & A11Y | 2 | 20 |
| 3 | Angular, Nx, Debugging | 3 | 20 |
| 4 | Fullstack (NestJS), Architecture, LLM Engineering | 4 | 20 |
| 5 | Deployment, Scale & Optimization | 5 | 20 |

Each pillar is owned by the judge who taught that day — see [[People]].

## What this means for us
- **Balance beats specialization** — even 20/20/20/20/20; a frontend-only team caps
  at ~80 and risks [[Criterion Zero]].
- **A11Y (Day 2)** and **Deployment (Day 5)** are each a full 20% most teams
  under-invest in — cheap, high-value points ([[Winning Playbook]]).
- **Pitch to "Client + Investor"** — business value + market readiness, not just tech.

## Still unknown
- The **specific assigned domain task** per team (Criterion Zero target).
- **Prizes.**
