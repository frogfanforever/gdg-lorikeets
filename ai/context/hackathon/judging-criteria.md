## The framing
At the final hackathon your team faces a **judging panel playing the role of
Client + Potential Investor**. You are scored on how effectively you used the
knowledge from *each day* of the marathon to turn a raw problem into a
**finished, market-ready, working product**.

## ⚠️ Criterion Zero (necessary condition — a gate, not points)
> The app **must fulfill the assigned domain task**. AI tools and implemented
> algorithms must correctly and logically process the problem and return
> substantively correct, sensible solutions. If the system does not fulfill the
> basic business function (i.e. does not solve the assigned problem), the
> project **fails Client verification.**

**Translation: a polished demo that doesn't actually solve the assigned domain
problem is disqualified before points are even counted.** Correct, working core
function first — everything else second.

## Scoring: 100 pts = 5 pillars × 20 pts (one per workshop day)

| # | Pillar                                             | Day | Max |
|---|----------------------------------------------------|-----|-----|
| 1 | **Product Design & MVP**                           | 1   | 20  |
| 2 | **UI/UX & A11Y** (accessibility)                   | 2   | 20  |
| 3 | **Angular, NX, Debugging**                         | 3   | 20  |
| 4 | **Fullstack (NestJS), Architecture, LLM Engineering** | 4 | 20  |
| 5 | **Deployment, Scale & Optimization**               | 5   | 20  |

## What this means for us (Lorikeets)
- **Balance beats specialization.** Points are split evenly 20/20/20/20/20, so a
  team that's great at frontend but never deploys caps at ~80 and likely trips
  Criterion Zero. Cover **all five** pillars.
- **A11Y is a full 20% pillar** (Day 2) — most teams will under-invest here. A
  deliberate accessibility pass is cheap, high-marginal-value points.
- **Deployment is 20%** — a live, deployed product (Cloud Run) is worth as much
  as the entire frontend. Deploy early, not in the last hour.
- **Pitch to "Client + Investor."** Frame the demo as business value + market
  readiness, not just tech. Show the problem solved, then the product working.
- Judges Perdek (Day 1), Romański (Day 3), Mysior (Day 5) each own a pillar —
  see `people.md`.
